Project Title: Power Law Forecasting for Sales & Production Optimization

Overview
This project analyzes monthly global sales and production data from December 2023 to November 2025 (24 observations). The dataset shows moderate fluctuations without extreme outliers, making it suitable for trend-based forecasting.

Forecasting Method
A power law trend model was used. The data was log-transformed and linear regression was applied to estimate the model parameters. The fitted model was then used to generate predictions for the test dataset.

Model Performance
The model achieved a Mean Absolute Percentage Error (MAPE) of 2.48%. This indicates that, on average, predictions deviate from actual values by only 2.48%. The forecast closely follows the real sales trend, showing strong model reliability.

Business Impact

•	Safety Stock Optimization:
Forecast accuracy enables a 30–50% reduction in safety stock.
For an average demand of ~260,000 units, this corresponds to a reduction of ~8,000–13,000 units, lowering holding costs.

•	Production Planning:
Improved forecasting reduces error from ~5% to 2.48%, allowing tighter production planning and reducing overproduction by ~2,500 units per cycle.

•	Inventory Efficiency:
Better alignment of supply and demand improves inventory turnover from ~4.0 to ~4.5 times per year.

•	Cost Savings:
o	Holding cost reduction: ~$4,000–$6,500 per month
o	Expedited shipping reduction: 15–25% annually
o	Procurement efficiency gains: 1–3% through better planning and contracts

Conclusion
The power law forecasting model significantly improves demand prediction accuracy and enables measurable supply chain cost reductions and operational efficiency improvements.
